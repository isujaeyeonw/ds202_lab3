`%notin%` <- Negate(`%in%`)

happiness1 = happiness %>% 
  filter(edu %notin% c("No answer", "Don't know")) %>%
  group_by(party) %>%
  summarise(mean_edu = mean(as.numeric(edu), na.rm = TRUE)) %>%
  arrange(desc(mean_edu))

happiness1 %>%
  ggplot(aes(x = reorder(party, -mean_edu), weight = mean_edu, fill = party)) +
  geom_bar() +

  ggtitle('Barplot of Average Years of Education for Each Political Party Affiliation') +
  xlab('Party Affiliation') +
  ylab('Average Years of Education')

For this analysis, I excluded the answers No answer and Dont know in the edu variable. People who did not answer or did not know their party affiliation have the highest average years of education (around 9 years). People in strong Democratic party affiliation had the next highest mean years of education of 8.8 years, followed by Other party, Not strong democrat, Strong republican, Independent, Independent near democrat, Not strong republican, and Independent near republican. The people in Independent near Republican group showed the lowest average years of education of 7.9 years.
